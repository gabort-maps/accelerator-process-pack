Executive Project Proposal

Standardised Accelerator Operating Model, updated for the accepted mentoring and reporting model

Prepared for accelerator programme leadership and partners. This executive note now aligns the workflow pages, the standalone Reporting layer page, and the latest reporting benchmark into one coherent operating-model proposal.

Unified Equity / Fast Track model Founder-owned reporting One core report object Manual first, automation-ready later

Purpose

Approve a project to design and implement a standardised, auditable, and scalable operating model for the accelerator programme. The accepted target state is no longer just a simple Equity / Fast Track fork. It now includes a formal mentoring-delivery layer and a separate standalone Reporting layer that keeps cadence, KPI governance, quality control, audience logic, and escalation consistent across the programme.

Core intent: create one formal operating model, one standard terminology set, one mandatory template and workbook suite, and one reporting and KPI framework with audience-specific views.

Why now

Scaling risk

Reliance on individual judgement creates inconsistency across startups, mentors, partners, and cohorts. That drift grows materially once the programme scales to 20–30 startups per cohort.

Comparability risk

Outputs are not yet guaranteed to be comparable or traceable across the programme, which weakens recommendation quality, support allocation, and later investor facilitation.

Visibility risk

Mentors and programme staff need clearer operational visibility with less administration. The accepted model solves for that through structured matching, delivery discipline, and a dedicated Reporting layer.

Benchmark signal

The reporting benchmark reviewed across leading EU and US accelerators shows a consistent pattern: best-in-class programmes run a higher-frequency internal operating cadence alongside a lower-frequency investor cadence, keep the operating update short and action-oriented, tie metrics review directly to mentoring, and use tiered disclosure by audience. The same benchmark also points to monthly investor communication during a raise, rather than universal investor reporting for every startup at all times.

Internal programme cadence

Usually weekly or every two weeks, discussion-led, and tied directly to partner, mentor, or accountability sessions.

Investor-facing cadence

Investor-facing monthly reporting should be activated only for startups that are actively fundraising; otherwise reporting remains internal and management-facing.

Common format

Short, comparable, and action-oriented, built around KPI movement, blockers, asks, and next priorities rather than long narrative reporting.

Disclosure boundary

Detailed operational data stays with programme staff and selected mentors. Outside audiences see a curated view, not the full internal operating record.

Our accepted model follows that logic but adapts it to track context: Fast Track uses a weekly pulse by default, Equity uses a biweekly pulse by default, the investor-facing monthly view is triggered only when a startup is actively fundraising, and the full detailed rules live on the standalone Reporting layer page.

Proposed operating approach

Unified model
One reusable operating model from startup selection to investor facilitation, with an early Equity / Fast Track fork and common data structures behind both paths.
Mentoring layer
A formal mentor-matching and mentoring-delivery sequence with an activation package, milestone-based work plan, session records, light progress signal, stage-gate build-up, and closure / continuation outcomes.
Reporting layer
One standalone cross-track Reporting layer page with one core report object, audience-controlled views, track-based cadence, accelerator quality control, and staged escalation for repeated weak or missing reporting.
Operating principle
Founder-owned reporting in both tracks, mentor validation only where Equity logic requires it, and accelerator completeness / consistency control in both tracks.
Scope in
Unified process design, Equity Track, Fast Track, mentor matching, mentoring delivery, Reporting layer, mandatory templates and workbooks, KPI framework, and HTML-ready reference logic.
Scope out
Full software platform build, deep integrations, investor CRM, or an external mentor marketplace at this stage.

Reference model in the HTML package

The Accelerator Process Flow remains the visual backbone of the project. The written proposal defines scope, logic, and governance; the HTML package shows how they connect operationally.

Workflow pages

Full overview, Equity Track detail, Mentoring delivery, and Fast Track detail stay page-specific and readable. Reporting appears there only as local touchpoints and short page summaries.

Reporting source of truth

The standalone Reporting layer tab holds the full cadence, KPI baseline, QC, audience-view, investor-view, and escalation logic.

Implementation posture

Manual first, automation-ready later. The current HTML package acts as the living reference architecture for later tooling, templates, and light integration.

Reporting and mentoring implications

  • Mentoring remains the Equity Track continuation, not a separate standalone process.
  • The mentoring detailed page stays execution-focused and now uses Light progress signal rather than a hardcoded weekly update node.
  • Detailed cadence, KPI baseline control, quality control, investor-view logic, and escalation now belong to the standalone Reporting layer page.
  • This keeps workflow pages readable while preserving one coherent reporting standard across the programme.

Executive project proposal

This proposal seeks approval to design and implement a standardised, auditable, and scalable operating model for the accelerator programme. The current accepted design is no longer just a simple Equity / Fast Track fork. It now includes a formal mentoring-delivery layer and a separate standalone Reporting layer that keeps cadence, KPI governance, quality control, audience logic, and escalation consistent across the programme.

Why this project is needed

  • Reliance on individual judgement creates inconsistency across startups, mentors, and cohorts.
  • Outputs are not yet guaranteed to be comparable or traceable across the programme.
  • Mentors and programme staff need clearer operational visibility with less administration.
  • Scaling to 20–30 startups per cohort increases process drift unless a formal model is approved.

Target outcome

  • One approved operating model.
  • One standard terminology set.
  • One mandatory template and workbook suite.
  • One reporting and KPI framework with one core report object and audience-specific views.
  • One light automation-ready architecture.

Scope summary

In scope
Unified process design, Equity Track, Fast Track, mentor matching, mentoring delivery, Reporting layer, mandatory templates and workbooks, KPI framework, and HTML-ready reference logic.
Out of scope
Full software platform build, deep integrations, investor CRM, or an external mentor marketplace at this stage.

Project objective

Design and implement a reusable accelerator operating model that standardises the startup journey from programme entry to investor facilitation, preserves consistency across reviewers, programme staff, mentors, and decision-makers, creates an auditable record of key inputs, outputs, and major decisions, and minimises administrative burden.

Design principles

Standardised, not rigid

Use structured process with controlled flexibility and guided overrides.

Auditability by design

Major decisions must be visible and defensible.

Low mentor administration

Mentors focus on support, not process management.

Startup-level logic inside cohort constraints

The system must work per startup and across the cohort.

Reusable model

The model should be suitable for future cohorts without redesign.

Scope definition

Formal start point
Startup selected into the accelerator.
Formal end point
Investor facilitation completed.
Target scale
Up to 20–30 startups per cohort.

Target operating model

The target model is one unified process with an early fork, supported by common data structures, mandatory templates, a controlled vocabulary, and a standalone Reporting layer page inside the HTML reference.

Shared entry layer

Startup selected into programme, self-assessment, first 1:1 review, and common base data capture.

Equity Track

Diagnosis of mentoring and support needs, startup demand profile and package, mentor supply standardisation and matching, then assignment, review, or gap handling.

Fast Track

Investor-readiness pre-check, evidence and DD / data-room requirements, conditional remediation where gaps exist, optional light support where justified, and investor facilitation handoff.

Reporting layer

Standalone cross-track reporting layer with one core report object, audience-controlled views, track-based cadence, monthly internal view, and investor-facing monthly view only when fundraising.

Mentor matching system

Structured startup demand profiles and structured mentor supply profiles enable matching that is more consistent, auditable, and scalable.

  • Fit ranking on primary need, secondary need, sector, stage, and engagement type.
  • Feasibility filtering on availability, capacity, affordability, and conflicts.
  • Assignment boundary rules to avoid fragmented allocations.
  • Final decision through assign, review, or gap handling.

Mentoring delivery and reporting layers

The accepted model now combines a formal mentoring-delivery sequence with a separate reporting source-of-truth page in the HTML package.

  • Mentoring starts through a three-party activation and a formal activation package.
  • The work plan is milestone-based and traceable.
  • The startup drafts session records; the mentor confirms them.
  • Between sessions, the system uses a Light progress signal rather than hardcoding a universal weekly mentoring update.
  • Detailed cadence, quality control, KPI baseline control, investor-view logic, and escalation rules belong to the standalone Reporting layer page.
  • Founder-owned reporting remains the core principle across both tracks.

Reporting layer summary

Core object
One core report object with audience-controlled views.
Cadence
Fast Track weekly pulse; Equity biweekly pulse by default.
Investor view
Monthly and only for startups actively fundraising.
KPI timing
Agree after diagnosis / assignment / review plus short calibration.
Quality control
Accelerator completeness / consistency gate; mentor validation only in Equity.
Escalation
Staged response to repeated weak or missing reporting.

Workstreams

WS1 Operating model design

Define target-state process and branch logic. Typical outputs: approved operating model, step logic, exception paths.

WS2 Template and workbook standardisation

Translate process into mandatory operating tools. Typical outputs: startup workbook, mentor master, DD inventory, reporting structures.

WS3 Mentoring system design

Define diagnosis, profiles, matching, activation, delivery, reporting touchpoints, review, and gap handling.

WS4 Fast Track and investor-readiness design

Define readiness, remediation, facilitation, and fit-ready support rhythm.

WS5 Reporting and KPI layer

Define performance, consistency, and audience-view logic. Typical outputs: core report object, KPI governance, management view, audit traceability.

WS6 Light automation readiness

Prepare for future scaling without building software now. Typical outputs: data fields, export points, minimum integration requirements.

Proposed phasing

Phase 1. Project definition and detailed design

Convert the concept into an approved operating model. Outputs: approved scope, flow, vocabulary, mentoring design, reporting design, KPI framework.

Phase 2. Tooling and template build

Build the operating pack. Outputs: workbook suite, template set, pilot-ready tools, activation and reporting materials.

Phase 3. Pilot and refinement

Test the model on live use cases. Outputs: revised templates, corrected process logic, clarified exceptions.

Phase 4. Rollout

Adopt the model as standard for future cohorts. Outputs: operating pack, onboarding guidance, reporting cadence, automation backlog.

Success criteria

Process quality
Consistent and defensible outputs across actors. Illustrative measures: comparability of structured outputs; share of major decisions supported by standard inputs.
Speed and efficiency
Timely progression without excess admin. Illustrative measures: turnaround from selection to recommendation; turnaround from review to assignment / readiness.
Startup outcomes
Visible progression through support and facilitation. Illustrative measures: activation of support; progression to investor readiness; progression to investor facilitation.
Mentor efficiency
Mentor time spent on value, not administration. Illustrative measures: admin burden reduction; mentor hours on support vs coordination; reassignment rate.
Mentoring visibility
The accelerator can see progress and risk without heavy manual chasing. Illustrative measures: session-capture rate; progress-signal completion; red-flag response time; stage-gate package completeness.

Risks and assumptions

  • Risk: the project grows beyond initial expectations. Response: phase the work and keep approval gates explicit.
  • Risk: the process becomes too complex. Response: keep the design operator-led, page-specific, and tool-backed.
  • Risk: standardisation creates unnecessary overhead. Response: preserve controlled flexibility and low mentor administration.
  • Assumption: existing drafts remain exploratory input, not the approved model.
  • Assumption: the flowchart remains the visual backbone and is updated in parallel with the written scope.
  • Assumption: the reporting layer may begin manually before later automation choices are made.
